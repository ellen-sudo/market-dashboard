Market Analysis

The spring market in Cary, Apex, Holly Springs, and Fuquay-Varina is sending buyers a clear signal: hesitation is costly. This week's snapshot — and note, this is one week of data, not a full month — shows 128 new listings entering the market against 112 new pendings. That ratio tells you almost everything. Inventory is being absorbed nearly as fast as it arrives, with homes averaging just 10 days on market compared to 4 days last year. The market has more supply than last spring, but buyers are just as hungry — and the competition in the best locations is fierce.

What does fierce look like in practice? A colleague shared this week that her client offered $200,000 over list price with $100,000 due diligence on a property Inside the Beltline just over $1 million — and still lost the bid. That is not an outlier story. That is what is happening at the upper end of the most desirable locations right now. Multiple-offer situations are occurring throughout the area, but they are especially intense in the hottest pockets. If you have been waiting for the market to "calm down," this week's data suggests spring 2025 is not going to give you that window.

The practical takeaway for buyers: get your financing locked and your priorities clear before you step foot in a home you love. Know your due diligence number in advance. Have a plan for moving quickly if the right property appears, because waiting even a day or two to "think about it" may mean losing it entirely. Contact Harmony Realty — we are watching this market in real time and we know which neighborhoods and price points are seeing the sharpest competition right now.

If you have been thinking about selling this spring, the data from this week across Cary, Apex, Holly Springs, and Fuquay-Varina should give you real confidence — with one important condition attached. The sellers winning right now are winning decisively: 112 homes went under contract against just 128 new listings this week. That absorption rate is extraordinary. Well-prepared, accurately priced homes are not sitting. They are attracting multiple offers, sometimes within days of hitting the market, and in the hottest locations, buyers are waiving contingencies and offering significant sums above asking.

But the other side of this market is just as real. 18 homes expired without selling this week, compared to just 8 in the same period last year. Those are not bad-luck stories. Those are homes that entered the market without adequate preparation, were overpriced, or had issues that today's buyers — experienced, discerning, and unwilling to take on problems — simply passed over. The gap between a home that sells in a week with multiple offers and a home that expires without a single offer has never been wider. Both outcomes are available to you, and the difference comes down almost entirely to preparation and honest pricing.

The opportunity this spring is real. Closed sales jumped to 74 this week from 49 last year — a 51% increase — which tells you there is genuine buyer demand being converted into sales. But those conversions are happening for the sellers who prepared their homes properly and entered the market at a price grounded in current data, not wishful thinking. We will tell you exactly what your home is worth in this market, what — if anything — needs to be addressed before you list, and how to position your property to be one of the homes buyers compete for, not one of the homes they pass by.

This week's data snapshot from Cary, Apex, Holly Springs, and Fuquay-Varina captures something that a monthly report often smooths over: the spring market is heating up sharply, and it is happening right now. 128 new listings came to market this week. 112 of them went pending. That is not a balanced market — that is a market where demand is almost perfectly matching new supply in real time, leaving very little inventory accumulation for buyers who are not already engaged and ready to move.

Median days on market stand at 10 days this week, up from 4 days in the same period last year. On the surface that looks like a cooling — and in some segments it is. But the on-the-ground picture is more nuanced. The homes that are prepared, priced accurately, and located in the highest-demand areas are still generating immediate and intense competition. A property Inside the Beltline, just over $1 million, received an offer $200,000 over list price with $100,000 due diligence — and the buyer still lost. Multiple-offer situations are not isolated events this spring; they are the expected outcome for well-positioned homes.

The market is also bifurcating sharply. Closed sales surged 51% year over year — 74 this week versus 49 last year — reflecting strong conversion of motivated buyers on the right homes. At the same time, expired listings more than doubled, from 8 to 18 this week. These two facts coexist in the same market because condition and pricing are determining everything. Buyers in 2025 are experienced and selective. They have more inventory to choose from than last year, and they are concentrating their attention — and their aggressive offers — on a narrow slice of that inventory. The Triangle market remains one of the most fundamentally healthy in the country, and this spring is proving it.
